Our Historic Sites

Dey Farm - Monroe Township

Imagine a classic American farmstead where life moved at the pace of the seasons. The Dey Farm, with its sturdy home first built in 1820 and iconic red barns, is a living snapshot of that era. For generations, this 42-acre property was the heart of the Dey family's world. Here, they raised families, tended to animals, and cultivated vast fields of potatoes destined for local markets. Today, the Dey Farm stands as the proud centerpiece of a historic hamlet, offering a rare and authentic chance to connect with Monroe's rich, rural past.
